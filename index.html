Damien Chazelle

An American Director

Damien Chazelle is set up to mark history as the worlds youngest recepitent of the Oscar's Best Director reward for the year of 2016. How did his amazing and inspiring career begin?

A Time Line of Damien Chazelle's life

  • On January 19th, 1985 Damien was born in Princeton New Jersey
  • During high school at Princeton High film making was Damien's first love but leaned toward drumming in his high school's jazz band
  • Damien has spoken about his jazz band's instructor stating that the instructor was so intense that he led to the inspiration to the character Terence Fletcher in Damien's first major film "Whiplash"
  • In 2007 Damien Chazelle graduated from Harvard University with a degree in Filmaking and Environmental Studies
  • Damien made his first debut as a director in 2009 with his film that is titled as "Guy and Madeline on a Park Bench"
  • In 2013 Damien received the Sundance Film Festival Short Prize for his short film "Whiplash"
  • After the hype and love for his short film Damien received enough recognition and funding to create "Whiplash" into a full feature film received high praise from critics everywhere
  • Now, in 2016, his biggest hit so far, "La La Land," is receiving rave reviews from critics and people all across the globe. "La La Land" won 7 Golden Globes and is nominated for 17 Academy Awards
